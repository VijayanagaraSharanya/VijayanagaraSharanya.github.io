Graph Showing Most Selected Ship Mode

Analyzing trends in shipping mode preferences over time could provide insights into shifting customer behavior and preferences. Regularly reviewing shipping mode performance will help inform logistics strategies and promotional efforts

Analysis of the graph:

The Standard Class The Standard Class shipping mode has the highest sales volume, exceeding 1.4 million. This indicates that most customers prefer this shipping method, likely due to its balance of cost and delivery time

The Second Class shipping mode accounts for a significant amount of sales, but it is notably less than Standard Class. This could imply that while customers are willing to opt for a slower delivery option, they still prioritize affordability

The First Class shipping mode shows a moderate level of sales, while Same Day shipping has the least sales. This could suggest that customers are not inclined to pay for expedited shipping unless necessary.

Insights gained and Steps to be followed:

Standard Class shipping should continue to be the main focus due to its widespread use. This means that prompt delivery and effective logistics management are essential to its success.
Marketing for Second Class: Given that Second Class also contributes significantly to sales, marketing initiatives should highlight the advantages of this affordable choice in order to draw in more clients.
Examine First Class and Same Day delivery: Given that these delivery options have fewer sales, you could want to do research or customer surveys to find out what obstacles exist for these shipping options. Improving the value proposition for expedited delivery choices may persuade clients to select them.

Graph Showing Sales by customer name and category


Technology appears to be the dominant category in terms of total sales, followed by Office Supplies and Furniture. Furniture has the smallest overall sales compared to the other two categories.

Technology has a few customers with exceptionally high sales, potentially indicating the presence of large-scale projects or bulk purchases. Sales Trends Over Time: Analyzing sales data over time could identify seasonal patterns, growth trends, and potential anomalies

Graph showing Trend in Sales over Years

The chart shows a clear upward trend in sales from 2015 to 2018. This indicates a positive growth in sales over the period.

2015: Sales were at their highest point during this year.
2016: Sales experienced a significant drop compared to 2015.
2017: Sales showed a recovery from the previous year, but remained below 2015 levels.
2018: Sales reached a new peak, surpassing the 2015 high.
